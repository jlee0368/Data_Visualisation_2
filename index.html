Malaysia MyRTKnet Network


What are they? üì°


MyRTKnet is Malaysia's nationwide Real-Time Kinematic GNSS reference station network, maintained by the Department of Survey and Mapping Malaysia (JUPEM). Its goal is to provide real-time GNSS corrections so that users (surveyors, GIS systems, field users) can get centimeter-level accuracy continuously across the country.

  • Permanent reference stations are distributed across Malaysia
  • These stations stream GNSS observations continuously to a central control center at JUPEM
  • The control center processes corrections and generates network correction streams
  • Users connect via internet/data link to receive these corrections real time

Terrain & Siting Elevation



The histogram of MyRTKnet station heights shows a strong skew toward low-lying elevations. Over 80% of stations are sited below 200 m above mean sea level, reflecting the concentration of Malaysia's urban and coastal population centers. Only a handful of stations exceed 600 m, representing those positioned on upland or interior terrain (notably in Pahang and Sabah). This elevation distribution confirms that the network primarily supports lowland users, where most geospatial, cadastral, and engineering activities occur.


The box plot highlights how station elevation varies geographically. Sabah and Pahang exhibit the widest elevation ranges, with stations extending above 1,000 m, indicative of deployment across rugged, mountainous terrain. Most west-coast states (Selangor, Negeri Sembilan, Melaka, Johor) cluster tightly below 100 m, emphasizing accessibility and ease of maintenance. Sarawak stations remain relatively low but geographically dispersed, mirroring the state's broad but gently undulating terrain. Together, these visuals reveal how terrain directly shapes siting strategy: states with mountainous interiors require elevated installations for signal visibility and network continuity, while flat coastal states achieve full coverage from lower elevations.

Toward a Resilient MyRTKnet


Building a resilient MyRTKnet involves not only expanding coverage but also ensuring real-time fault monitoring and interoperability with next-generation GNSS constellations. Continued investment in site upgrades, power reliability, and data-sharing infrastructure will ensure the network supports precise positioning disaster resilience, and smart nation initiatives for years to come.
