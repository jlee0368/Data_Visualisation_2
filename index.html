Malaysia MyRTKnet Network


What are they? üì°


MyRTKnet is Malaysia's nationwide Real-Time Kinematic GNSS reference station network, maintained by the Department of Survey and Mapping Malaysia (JUPEM). Its goal is to provide real-time GNSS corrections so that users (surveyors, GIS systems, field users) can get centimeter-level accuracy continuously across the country.

How it works? üõ∞Ô∏è


  • Permanent reference stations are distributed across Malaysia
  • These stations stream GNSS observations continuously to a central control center at JUPEM
  • The control center processes corrections and generates network correction streams
  • Users connect via internet/data link to receive these corrections real time

Network Overview ‚Äî Stations & Major Cities



The MyRTKnet reference stations are strategically distributed across both Peninsular Malaysia and East Malaysia (Sabah and Sarawak). Every major region is covered, confirming that JUPEM's network design ensures consistent nationwide GNSS correction services. Coverage density is higher along the west coast corridor of Peninsular Malaysia, aligning with population and economic centers like Klang Valley, Penang and Johor Bahru.

The chart reveals clear geographic disparities in station remoteness across Malaysia. Sabah and Sarawak stand out with median distances exceeding 150 km, and wide Q1-Q3 ranges, meaning many stations there are far from urban centers. In contrast, states in Peninsular Malaysia, especially Selangor, Negeri Sembilan, Perak, Pulau Pinang, and Melaka, have much smaller distances (typically below 50 km), showing that their reference stations are well integrated into city networks. Melaka has the smallest median distance, possibly due to Melaka being a small state.


Terrain & Siting Elevation



The histogram of MyRTKnet station heights shows a strong skew toward low-lying elevations. Over 80% of stations are sited below 200 m above mean sea level, reflecting the concentration of Malaysia's urban and coastal population centers. Only a handful of stations exceed 600 m, representing those positioned on upland or interior terrain (notably in Pahang and Sabah).


The box plot highlights how station elevation varies geographically. Sabah and Pahang exhibit the widest elevation ranges, with stations extending above 1,000 m, indicative of deployment across rugged, mountainous terrain. Most west-coast states (Selangor, Negeri Sembilan, Melaka, Johor) cluster tightly below 100 m, emphasizing accessibility and ease of maintenance.

Toward a Resilient MyRTKnet


Building a resilient MyRTKnet involves not only expanding coverage but also ensuring real-time fault monitoring and interoperability with next-generation GNSS constellations. Continued investment in site upgrades, power reliability, and data-sharing infrastructure will ensure the network supports precise positioning disaster resilience, and smart nation initiatives for years to come.
